Who Needs Training?

Cyber scams impact everyone — from 8-year-olds on TikTok to 80-year-olds answering the phone. No age group is safe. No income level is immune.

⚠️

Phishing Emails

Fake emails impersonating banks, IRS, Amazon — tricking you into giving up credentials.

📱

Smishing (Text Scams)

Fraudulent package alerts, banking warnings, and fake links via SMS targeting smartphones.

📞

Vishing (Voice Scams)

Callers posing as law enforcement, tech support, or government — using fear to steal.

🎯

Psychological Manipulation

Exploiting trust, urgency, fear, and emotion. Social engineering attacks your mind, not software.

📩

Romance & Emergency Scams

Fake relationships and "emergency family" calls designed to drain savings and retirement.

Know Your Enemy

Scams We Train Against

Our training covers the full spectrum of social engineering tactics targeting Americans today.

Email

Phishing

Fake emails impersonating trusted organizations to steal credentials and financial info.

  • Bank account verification
  • IRS tax refund notices
  • Package delivery alerts
  • Password reset requests
Text / SMS

Smishing

Text attacks exploiting the trust people place in mobile communications.

  • Fake delivery notices
  • Fraudulent banking alerts
  • Suspicious account warnings
  • Fake verification codes
Phone Call

Vishing

Voice calls impersonating institutions to pressure victims into immediate action.

  • Tech support scams
  • Government impersonation
  • Law enforcement threats
  • Investment fraud calls
Psychological

Social Engineering

Direct manipulation of trust, fear, urgency, and emotional vulnerability.

  • Emergency family scams
  • Romance scams
  • Investment fraud
  • Charity impersonation
Financial

Financial Fraud

Schemes targeting savings, retirement, credit, and digital assets.

  • Wire transfer scams
  • Cryptocurrency fraud
  • Gift card demands
  • Lottery & prize scams
Corporate

Business Attacks

Targeted attacks causing downtime, data breaches, and major financial losses.

  • CEO impersonation
  • Invoice fraud
  • Credential theft
  • Ransomware entry points
